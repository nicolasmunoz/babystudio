## Pricing Philosophy

### Core Principles

**1. Value-based, not cost-based.**
We price against the value we deliver -- measured by what the customer would otherwise spend on traditional baby content production. Our pricing reflects the outcome (governed, photorealistic, product-integrated infant imagery with perpetual rights) not the input cost of AI generation.

**2. Anchored to traditional production costs.**
Every pricing conversation starts with the customer's current spend. Our prices are meaningful only in relation to the $2,500-$8,000+ per-image cost of traditional baby photoshoots. We never present pricing in isolation.

**3. Premium positioning within AI.**
We are not the cheapest AI imagery option and we do not intend to be. General-purpose AI platforms offer images for $50-$500. We charge $500-$1,500 because we deliver baby-specific governance, product integration, compliance documentation, and perpetual usage rights. The premium reflects specialization, not overhead.

**4. Never compete on cheapest.**
If a prospect is shopping purely on price, they are not our customer. We compete on total value: quality, governance, compliance, speed, rights, and cost reduction relative to traditional production. If a prospect compares us to stock photography pricing, we redirect the conversation to product integration and trust-building capability that stock cannot provide.

**5. Transparent and all-inclusive.**
Our per-image price includes governance, compliance documentation, perpetual usage rights, and standard revisions. There are no hidden fees for compliance, no separate rights charges, no surprise post-production costs. What we quote is what the customer pays.

**6. Pricing rewards commitment.**
Volume and long-term engagement earn better per-image rates. This aligns our interests with our customers' -- we want ongoing relationships, not one-off transactions.

## Volume Discounts

Volume discounts apply to cumulative image count within a single project or across a 12-month commitment.

| Annual Volume | Discount | Effective Price Range |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|
| 10-24 images | 0% (standard Starter pricing) | $500-$750/image |
| 25-49 images | 5% | $475-$950/image |
| 50-99 images | 10% | $450-$900/image |
| 100-199 images | 15% | $425-$850/image |
| 200-499 images | 20% | $400-$800/image |
| 500+ images | Custom (typically 25-30%) | Negotiated |

**Volume discount rules:**
- Discounts apply to the entire order at the highest qualifying tier, not incrementally
- Annual commitments lock in volume pricing for the full year regardless of project-by-project volume
- Unused image credits from annual commitments do not roll over
- Volume discounts stack with tier pricing but not with pilot pricing

## Pilot Project Pricing

### Purpose

Pilots exist to prove value with minimal risk. They are not loss leaders -- they are structured proof-of-concept engagements designed to demonstrate quality, governance, and workflow fit before a larger commitment.

### Pilot Structure

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Discount** | 30-40% off standard tier pricing |
| **Image count** | 5-10 images |
| **Price range** | $2,500-$5,000 |
| **Scope** | Single SKU, single product, 2-3 demographic variations |
| **Duration** | 2-4 weeks (brief through delivery and evaluation) |
| **Includes** | Full governance and compliance documentation, post-pilot ROI analysis, internal presentation support for customer champion |
| **Success metrics** | Defined upfront with customer: quality assessment, compliance review pass, cost comparison, timeline comparison |

### Pilot Qualification Criteria

Offer a pilot when the prospect:
- Is a qualified buyer with authority or influence over production budget
- Has expressed genuine interest but needs proof before committing
- Has a specific upcoming production need (not exploring hypothetically)
- Is willing to define success criteria before the pilot begins
- Represents a viable long-term account (at least $25K+ annual potential)

Do not offer a pilot when the prospect:
- Is purely price-shopping with no intent to build a relationship
- Cannot define what success looks like
- Has no production need in the near term
- Would not convert to a paid engagement regardless of pilot results

### Pilot-to-Conversion Path

1. **Pre-pilot:** Define success criteria collaboratively. Document current production costs, timeline, and pain points for comparison.
2. **During pilot:** Deliver 5-10 images with full governance documentation. Maintain close communication.
3. **Post-pilot debrief:** Present results against agreed success criteria. Provide ROI analysis comparing pilot output to traditional production equivalents.
4. **Conversion proposal:** Present Growth or Enterprise tier proposal with volume commitment recommendation based on customer's annual production needs.
5. **Target:** Convert within 60 days of pilot completion.

## ROI Calculator

### Input Variables

| Variable | Description | How to Obtain |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| A | Annual number of baby product shoots | Customer's production schedule |
| B | Average fully-loaded cost per shoot | Customer's production budget (include all line items) |
| C | Average usable images per shoot | Customer's historical yield |
| D | Number of markets requiring localized imagery | Customer's market expansion plan |
| E | Cost per localized shoot | Customer's localization budget |
| F | Annual usage rights renegotiation costs | Customer's legal/procurement budget |
| G | Number of cancelled/rescheduled shoots per year | Customer's production history |
| H | Cost per cancellation/reschedule | Customer's estimate (typically $5K-$10K) |

### Formula

**Current annual production cost:**
Total Traditional = (A x B) + (D x E) + F + (G x H)

**babystudio annual cost:**
Total babystudio = (A x C) x babystudio per-image rate

**Annual savings:**
Savings = Total Traditional - Total babystudio

**ROI percentage:**
ROI = (Savings / Total babystudio) x 100

### Sample Calculation: Enterprise Customer

**Scenario:** Global diaper brand expanding across 8 markets.

| Input | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Annual shoots (A) | 12 |
| Cost per shoot (B) | $20,000 |
| Usable images per shoot (C) | 4 |
| Markets requiring localization (D) | 8 |
| Cost per localized shoot (E) | $20,000 |
| Annual rights renegotiation (F) | $30,000 |
| Cancelled shoots per year (G) | 3 |
| Cost per cancellation (H) | $8,000 |

**Current annual cost:**
(12 x $20,000) + (8 x $20,000) + $30,000 + (3 x $8,000) = $240,000 + $160,000 + $30,000 + $24,000 = **$454,000**

**babystudio annual cost:**
(12 x 4) x $1,200 (Enterprise tier average) = 48 images x $1,200 = **$57,600**
Plus localization: 8 markets x 5 images x $1,200 = **$48,000**
Total babystudio: **$105,600**

**Annual savings:** $454,000 - $105,600 = **$348,400**
**ROI:** ($348,400 / $105,600) x 100 = **330%**
**Cost reduction:** 77%

## Pricing Objection Responses

### 1. "That's more expensive than I expected for AI-generated images."

**Response:** That reaction makes sense if you are comparing us to general-purpose AI tools. We are not priced against Midjourney or DALL-E. We are priced against what you are currently spending on traditional baby product photography. A traditional shoot yields usable images at $2,500-$8,000 each after compliance costs. At $500-$1,500 per image, we deliver the same commercial-grade, product-integrated result with governance documentation and perpetual rights included. The relevant comparison is not "AI image price" -- it is "baby product imagery production cost."

### 2. "Stock images are $50. Why would I pay $500+?"

**Response:** Stock gives you a generic baby. It does not give you your product in a baby's hands, held correctly, used safely, across the demographics your customers represent. Parents buying baby products need to see the product being used -- that is what drives trust and conversion. Listings with product-integrated baby imagery convert 25-40% higher than product-only or generic stock listings. At $500 per image, you are not paying for a picture of a baby. You are paying for governed, product-specific imagery that sells your product. Stock cannot do that at any price.

### 3. "Our agency already does this for us at a reasonable rate."

**Response:** Your agency delivers excellent creative direction and campaign strategy. But the underlying production cost for baby shoots is structural -- welfare workers, permits, nurses, backup babies, and usage rights are not line items your agency can negotiate away. Those compliance costs exist regardless of who manages the shoot. babystudio eliminates that cost layer entirely. Many agencies partner with us to reduce their clients' production costs while maintaining or improving their own margins. We can work alongside your agency, not instead of them.

### 4. "We need to see if the quality justifies the price."

**Response:** Absolutely. Quality is the first question every brand asks, and it should be. We offer a pilot program specifically for this -- 5-10 images at 30-40% off standard pricing so you can evaluate quality, governance, and workflow fit with your actual product. If the quality does not meet your standard, you have your answer with minimal investment. If it does, you have a proven production partner at a fraction of your current cost.

### 5. "Can you do $300 per image if we commit to volume?"

**Response:** We appreciate the commitment signal, and volume absolutely earns better rates. Our volume discounts bring per-image pricing down significantly at scale. That said, our pricing reflects the baby-specific governance, compliance documentation, and perpetual rights that are included with every image. Pricing below our tiers would require removing elements -- like governance checks or compliance documentation -- that we believe are essential for commercial infant imagery. We would rather deliver the full value at a fair price than cut corners to hit a number.

### 6. "We're going to wait and see how AI imagery evolves before committing."

**Response:** That is a reasonable instinct. Here is the consideration: AI imagery quality has already crossed the commercial threshold -- it is indistinguishable from traditional photography in blind testing. What is evolving is governance, platform policy, and brand adoption norms. Brands that establish governed AI production processes now will have 12-18 months of operational advantage over brands that wait. A pilot lets you evaluate with minimal commitment while building internal capability and compliance documentation. Waiting has a cost -- competitors are not waiting.

### 7. "Our budget for this quarter is already allocated."

**Response:** Understood. Two thoughts: First, our pilot program is $2,500-$5,000, which is a fraction of a single traditional baby shoot. It may fit within existing discretionary or innovation budget. Second, if you are spending on traditional production this quarter, we can plan a side-by-side comparison for next quarter -- same brief, same product, babystudio output alongside your traditional shoot output. That gives you a direct quality and cost comparison to justify reallocation. We are happy to plan around your budget cycle.

## Terms Summary

### Usage Rights

- **Scope:** Perpetual, worldwide, across all media and platforms
- **Exclusions:** None -- usage rights are unlimited and never expire
- **Transfer:** Rights travel with the brand. If the customer is acquired, rights transfer to the acquiring entity
- **No renegotiation:** Usage rights are included in production pricing and are never subject to renegotiation, renewal fees, or expiration

### Revisions

| Tier | Included Revisions | Additional Revisions |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Starter | 2 rounds per image | $75-$100 per additional round |
| Growth | 3 rounds per image | $50-$75 per additional round |
| Enterprise | Unlimited within reasonable project scope | Included |

**What counts as a revision:** Changes to expression, positioning, demographic representation, background/context, product placement, lighting, or framing. Each revision round may include multiple change requests.

**What does not count as a revision:** New images beyond the original project scope (these are new orders). Fundamental changes to the product being depicted (requires new brief).

## How to Present Pricing

### Before You Show a Number

1. **Establish the anchor.** Always discuss the customer's current production costs first. Ask: "What does a typical baby photoshoot cost your team, fully loaded?" or "How much are you spending annually on baby product imagery?" The goal is for the customer to state a number that is higher than what you will propose.

2. **Quantify the pain.** Walk through the compliance tax: welfare workers, permits, nurses, backup babies, usage rights, reshoot risk. Help the customer calculate their true per-image cost (most underestimate by 30-50%).

3. **Introduce the comparison, not the price.** Frame pricing as: "Here is what the same outcome costs with babystudio" not "Here is our price." The comparison is the value proposition.

### When You Show the Number

4. **Lead with per-image pricing against their per-image cost.** "$1,000 per image versus the $3,500 per image you told me you are spending today" is more powerful than "$1,000 per image."

5. **Show the all-inclusive scope immediately.** Preempt "but what about..." questions by listing everything included: governance, compliance docs, perpetual rights, revisions.

6. **Use the ROI calculator.** Plug in their numbers live if possible. Seeing their specific savings makes the value tangible.

### After You Show the Number

7. **Pause.** Let the number land. Do not immediately justify or discount.

8. **Address the comparison, not the absolute.** If they react to the dollar amount, redirect to the percentage savings versus their current spend.

9. **Offer the pilot as the low-risk entry point.** If the full project price creates hesitation, the pilot at 30-40% discount reduces the commitment to $2,500-$5,000 -- a fraction of a single traditional shoot.

### Pricing Conversation Red Flags

- **Prospect only compares to stock or free AI tools.** They may not be our customer. Redirect to product integration and governance value. If they still anchor to $50-$200 per image, qualify whether they are a fit.
- **Prospect asks for discounts before understanding the scope.** Slow down. Return to value and total cost comparison before discussing discounts.
- **Prospect wants custom pricing for a small project.** Hold standard pricing. Customization is for Enterprise tier customers with demonstrated volume commitment.
- **Prospect says "I need to check with procurement."** This is normal for enterprise. Provide a formal proposal and cost comparison document for procurement review. Offer to join the procurement conversation.
