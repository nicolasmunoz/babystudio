The executive summary is the most important page in the proposal. Many decision-makers will read only this page. It must stand alone as a complete argument for the engagement.

### Process

Every babystudio engagement follows a five-step process designed for clarity, quality, and efficiency.

**Step 1: Discovery and Creative Direction (Days 1-2)**

We align on the creative brief, brand standards, and project specifications. For new customers, this includes brand guideline review and governance template creation. For returning customers, we confirm any updates to existing templates.

*Your involvement:* Complete the creative brief template (provided by babystudio), share product assets (photos, packaging files), confirm demographic and context requirements.

*babystudio delivers:* Finalized brief document, governance template confirmation, production timeline with specific delivery dates.

**Step 2: Creative Direction Approval (Day 2-3)**

Before production begins, we present creative direction for your approval: reference imagery demonstrating the intended style, lighting, composition, and product positioning. This ensures alignment before production resources are committed.

*Your involvement:* Review and approve creative direction. Provide feedback on any adjustments.

*babystudio delivers:* Creative direction document with reference imagery and production specifications.

**Step 3: Production (Days 3-5)**

AI-generated imagery is produced against the approved creative direction and governance templates. Every image passes our infant-specific governance checks: age-use alignment, safe positioning, expression calibration, and demographic accuracy.

*Your involvement:* None required during production. Status updates provided at agreed intervals.

*babystudio delivers:* First-round imagery for review.

**Step 4: Review and Revision (Days 5-7)**

You review the production output and provide feedback. Revision rounds are completed within 24-48 hours per round. Common revisions include expression adjustments, positioning refinements, background modifications, and demographic variations.

*Your involvement:* Review imagery, provide consolidated feedback per revision round.

*babystudio delivers:* Revised imagery per feedback, within 24-48 hours per round.

**Step 5: Final Delivery (Day 7-10)**

Final approved imagery is delivered in all specified formats with complete compliance documentation: governance verification records, AI disclosure guidance for each relevant platform, and perpetual usage rights confirmation.

*Your involvement:* Final approval sign-off.

*babystudio delivers:* Production-ready imagery in all formats, compliance documentation package, usage rights confirmation.

## Governance and Compliance

This section is standard across all proposals. It is particularly important for enterprise proposals where legal stakeholders will review.

### Standard Language

babystudio's governance framework is purpose-built for infant imagery in commercial contexts. Every image we produce is governed across four dimensions before delivery.

**Age-Use Alignment**
The depicted infant's age range matches the intended use range of the product being shown. A product designed for 6-9 month infants is depicted with an infant whose developmental characteristics -- motor skills, proportions, expressions -- accurately represent that age range. This prevents misrepresentation and ensures the imagery accurately communicates the product's intended audience.

**Safe Positioning**
Every product interaction depicted follows manufacturer guidelines and infant safety standards. Bottles are held at appropriate angles. Diapers fit naturally. Clothing is worn without restriction or hazard. No imagery depicts a product interaction that would raise safety concerns from a pediatric, regulatory, or common-sense perspective.

**Expression Calibration**
Infant expressions match developmental capabilities. Newborns do not smile broadly. Six-month-olds do not sit unassisted without support. Nine-month-olds do not walk. These developmental accuracy checks ensure the imagery is realistic and prevents the "uncanny" quality that undermines trust.

**Demographic Accuracy**
Ethnic and physical features are consistent, respectful, and representative without stereotyping or tokenization. Demographic representation is produced with the same governance rigor as every other production dimension.

### Compliance Documentation Deliverables

Every asset is delivered with:
- **Governance verification record:** Documented confirmation that the image passed all four governance checks
- **AI disclosure guidance:** Platform-specific recommendations for Meta, Amazon, Google, TikTok, and other relevant platforms, updated to current policy requirements
- **Usage rights confirmation:** Perpetual, worldwide usage rights documentation with no expiration, no renegotiation, and no platform or media restrictions
- **Production metadata:** Technical specifications, governance template version, and production parameters for audit trail purposes

### Legal Review Support

babystudio provides compliance documentation designed for legal team review. We are available to participate in legal review calls, answer governance methodology questions, and provide additional documentation as needed to support your internal approval process.

## Proposal Best Practices

### Before Writing

1. **Complete discovery first.** Never write a proposal without at least one substantive discovery conversation. The proposal should reflect what you learned, not what you assume.
2. **Confirm the decision-making process.** Know who will read the proposal, who decides, and what their evaluation criteria are. Tailor the document accordingly.
3. **Identify the internal champion.** The proposal should be easy for your champion to circulate and defend internally. Include materials they can use (executive summary, ROI analysis, compliance overview).

### While Writing

4. **Use the customer's language.** If they said "photoshoots," do not write "production sessions." If they said "our legal team is tough," address legal specifically. Mirror their vocabulary.
5. **Be specific, not comprehensive.** A proposal that addresses the customer's three top concerns deeply is more effective than one that addresses ten concerns superficially.
6. **Anchor every price to a comparison.** Never present a babystudio price without context. "$50,000" is abstract. "$50,000 versus your current $150,000 annual spend" is a value proposition.
7. **Anticipate objections.** If you know the customer has a specific concern (quality, legal, internal buy-in), address it proactively in the relevant section. Do not wait for them to raise it.

### After Sending

8. **Follow up with a walkthrough offer.** Do not assume the proposal speaks for itself. Offer to walk through it live, especially with stakeholders who were not in discovery conversations.
9. **Provide supporting materials.** Send the relevant one-pager and case study alongside the proposal. These are easier to forward internally than the full proposal.
10. **Set a specific next step.** The proposal should end with a clear recommended action and a proposed timeline. "Let us schedule a call next Tuesday to discuss" is better than "Let us know your thoughts."

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Sending a generic proposal.** If the customer's name could be swapped for any other customer and the proposal would still make sense, it is not customized enough.
- **Leading with technology.** The proposal should lead with the business problem and the business outcome. AI is the method, not the message.
- **Overloading with options.** Present one recommended engagement with a clear scope and price. Offer alternatives only if the customer asked for flexibility.
- **Skipping the executive summary.** Some stakeholders will only read this page. It must be complete and compelling on its own.
- **Forgetting the internal champion.** Include materials (one-page summary, ROI analysis, compliance overview) that your champion can use to advocate internally without needing to paraphrase the full proposal.
