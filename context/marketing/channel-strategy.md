# Channel Strategy

This document guides resource allocation and content distribution across marketing channels for babystudio. It defines where we show up, how we show up, what we publish on each channel, and how channels work together to move prospects through the buying journey.

## Channel Strategy Philosophy

babystudio is a pre-revenue company selling a specialized product to a niche B2B audience. This means three things for channel strategy:

1. **Depth over breadth.** We do not need to be everywhere. We need to be excellent on the channels where our buyers already spend time. Three channels done well outperform seven channels done adequately.

2. **Earned credibility over paid reach.** At this stage, trust is built through consistency and quality, not ad spend. Organic content that demonstrates expertise earns more durable attention than paid impressions that interrupt.

3. **Conversations over broadcasts.** Our deal sizes ($25K-$500K/year) justify high-touch engagement. Every channel strategy should create opportunities for one-to-one conversations, not just one-to-many broadcasts.

**Scoring methodology:**
- Impact Potential: How directly this channel can influence pipeline and revenue
- Effort Required: Time, skill, and resources needed for consistent execution
- Time to Results: How quickly we see measurable impact
- Persona Reach: How many of our three personas are accessible on this channel
- Funnel Stages Served: Which buying stages this channel supports
- Priority Score: Weighted composite (Impact 3x, Effort 2x inverse, Time 1x inverse, Reach 2x, Funnel 2x)

## Primary Channels (Immediate Focus)

These channels receive the majority of resources and attention from day one. They are the foundation of babystudio's go-to-market presence.

### LinkedIn (Organic)

**Role in strategy:** Primary thought leadership platform, persona targeting, community building, and direct conversation generation.

**Why it is our top channel:** LinkedIn is where all three of our personas spend professional time. Enterprise Emma observes passively. Startup Sam engages actively. Agency Alex contributes content and monitors trends. No other single channel gives us access to all three segments simultaneously. B2B buying decisions in our price range are influenced heavily by LinkedIn presence and credibility.

**Target audience:** All three personas -- Enterprise Emma (passive consumption, responds to data and thought leadership), Startup Sam (active engagement, responds to tactical content and peer proof), Agency Alex (active posting, responds to industry perspective and creative capability).

**Content types:**

| Content Format | Frequency | Pillar Alignment | Persona Focus |
|---------------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Text posts (thought leadership) | 2/week | Pillar 4, Pillar 1 | Enterprise Emma, Agency Alex |
| Text posts (tactical / problem-focused) | 1-2/week | Pillar 1, Pillar 3 | Startup Sam, Agency Alex |
| Carousel posts (visual, educational) | 1/week | Pillar 2, Pillar 3 | All personas |
| Behind-the-scenes posts | 1/week | Pillar 5 | Startup Sam, Agency Alex |
| Comment engagement | Daily | All pillars | All personas |
| DM conversations | As earned | N/A | Qualified prospects |
| Long-form articles | 1-2/month | Pillar 4, Pillar 2 | Enterprise Emma |

**Publishing cadence:** 4-5 posts per week. Consistency matters more than volume. Missing one post is better than publishing a mediocre one.

**Posting schedule:**
- Monday: Thought leadership or industry perspective (Pillar 4) -- sets the tone for the week
- Tuesday: Production problem content (Pillar 1) -- names a pain point with data or story
- Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes or governance (Pillar 5 or Pillar 2) -- builds trust mid-week
- Thursday: Business impact or data post (Pillar 3) -- provides conversion-focused evidence
- Friday: Lighter industry observation or engagement-focused post (Pillar 4) -- invites conversation

**Best posting times:** 8:00-9:30 AM ET (Tuesday-Thursday are highest engagement days for B2B).

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target (Month 1-3) | Target (Month 4-6) | Target (Month 7-12) |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Impressions per post | 500-1,000 | 1,000-3,000 | 3,000-10,000 |
| Engagement rate | 2-3% | 3-5% | 4-6% |
| Profile views per week | 50-100 | 100-300 | 300-1,000 |
| Connection requests sent | 20-30/week | 30-50/week | Maintain |
| DM conversations started | 3-5/week | 5-10/week | 10-20/week |
| Content-attributed meetings | 1-2/month | 3-5/month | 5-10/month |

**Resource allocation:** 35% of total content effort.

**LinkedIn-specific guidelines:**
- Every post should deliver value without requiring a click. Do not use LinkedIn as a teaser for blog posts.
- Open with a scroll-stopping first line. The first 2-3 lines determine whether the post is read.
- Use line breaks generously. Dense paragraphs die on LinkedIn.
- End with a question or clear takeaway, not a hard sell.
- Engage in comments on other people's content daily. Giving attention earns attention.
- DM outreach should follow genuine engagement, never cold. Comment on their content first.
- Never use "Exciting news!" or exclamation-driven openings. Lead with substance.
- Hashtags: 3-5 maximum. Use category-specific tags (#BabyProducts, #CPGMarketing, #EcommerceImagery) not generic ones (#AI, #Innovation).

### Email

**Role in strategy:** Direct relationship building, lead nurturing, meeting generation, and ongoing engagement with qualified prospects.

**Why it is critical:** Email is the channel that converts attention into conversations. LinkedIn creates awareness; email deepens it. Cold outreach reaches prospects who have not yet found us. Nurture sequences maintain momentum between touchpoints. The newsletter establishes recurring mindshare.

**Target audience:** Qualified leads across all segments. Cold outreach prioritizes Enterprise Emma and Agency Alex (higher deal values). Nurture and newsletter cover all segments.

**Email programs:**

#### Cold Outreach

**Purpose:** Generate meetings with qualified prospects who match our ICP.

**Sequence structure:** 5-touch sequence over 3 weeks.

| Touch | Timing | Content Focus | Pillar |
|-------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Email 1 | Day 0 | Lead with their specific pain point. Name the compliance tax or 20-minute window. | Pillar 1 |
| Email 2 | Day 3 | Share a relevant data point or insight. No pitch. | Pillar 3 or 4 |
| Email 3 | Day 7 | Brief case example or proof point. Ask a question. | Pillar 3 |
| Email 4 | Day 12 | Governance and compliance angle. Differentiation. | Pillar 2 |
| Email 5 | Day 18 | Direct ask. Offer a sample set with their product. | Pillar 5 |

**Volume:** 20-40 personalized cold emails per week (prioritize quality over volume).

**Personalization requirements:** Every cold email must reference something specific about the recipient -- a product they market, a challenge their company faces, a piece of content they published. No generic templates.

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 40-55% |
| Reply rate | 8-15% |
| Positive reply rate | 4-8% |
| Meetings booked per 100 emails | 3-6 |

#### Nurture Sequences

**Purpose:** Educate and build trust with leads who are aware but not yet ready to buy.

**Sequence structure:** 8-touch sequence over 8 weeks, then transition to newsletter.

| Touch | Timing | Content Focus | Pillar |
|-------|--------|---------------|--------|
| Email 1 | Week 1 | Welcome + core problem framing | Pillar 1 |
| Email 2 | Week 2 | The compliance tax deep dive | Pillar 1 |
| Email 3 | Week 3 | How governed AI imagery works | Pillar 2 |
| Email 4 | Week 4 | Cost comparison and ROI data | Pillar 3 |
| Email 5 | Week 5 | Governance framework and compliance documentation | Pillar 2 |
| Email 6 | Week 6 | Industry perspective -- where the category is heading | Pillar 4 |
| Email 7 | Week 7 | Behind the scenes -- how we built this | Pillar 5 |
| Email 8 | Week 8 | Invitation to next step (demo, sample set, conversation) | Pillar 3 |

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 35-45% |
| Click rate | 5-10% |
| Sequence completion rate | 40-60% |
| Conversion to meeting | 5-10% of sequence completers |

#### Newsletter

**Purpose:** Ongoing relationship maintenance, thought leadership, and top-of-mind positioning.

**Frequency:** Bi-weekly (every other Thursday).

**Format:**
- 1 featured insight (original thought leadership, 200-300 words)
- 1 recent blog post or content link
- 1 industry observation or data point
- 1 call to action (always low-pressure: "reply to this email," "read the full post," "see your product in babystudio imagery")

**Tone:** The most human and opinionated of all email formats. Reads like a knowledgeable colleague sharing what they are seeing in the category. Not a promotional digest.

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 30-40% |
| Click rate | 3-6% |
| Unsubscribe rate | <0.5% per send |
| Reply rate | 1-3% |

**Email resource allocation:** 25% of total content effort (split: 40% cold outreach, 30% nurture, 30% newsletter).

### Blog / Website

**Role in strategy:** SEO foundation, thought leadership depth, conversion hub, and content library for all other channels.

**Why it matters:** The blog is the engine that powers every other channel. LinkedIn posts are distilled from blog content. Email sequences link to blog posts. Sales conversations reference blog evidence. And organic search brings in prospects who are actively searching for solutions to the problems we solve.

**Target audience:** Problem-aware and solution-aware prospects across all segments. Blog content is optimized for search intent, which means it reaches prospects at the moment they are looking for answers.

**Content types:**

| Content Type | Frequency | Pillar | Primary Persona | Funnel Stage |
|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Problem deep dives | 1/month | Pillar 1 | All | Awareness |
| How-it-works / educational | 1/month | Pillar 2 | Enterprise, Agency | Consideration |
| ROI / data posts | 1/month | Pillar 3 | All | Consideration, Decision |
| Industry analysis | 1-2/quarter | Pillar 4 | Enterprise, Agency | Awareness |
| Behind-the-scenes | 1/quarter | Pillar 5 | DTC, Agency | Awareness, Consideration |
| Comparison posts | 1/quarter | Pillar 2, 3 | All | Consideration |
| Case studies | As available | Pillar 3 | All | Decision |
| Compliance guides | 1-2/quarter | Pillar 2 | Enterprise | Consideration |

**Publishing cadence:** 2-3 posts per month. Quality and depth over frequency. Every post should be the best thing written on its topic for our audience.

**Blog post standards:**
- Minimum 1,200 words for standard posts. 2,000-3,000 for pillar content.
- Every post targets at least one keyword cluster from the SEO Keyword Strategy.
- Every post includes at least one specific data point or proof point.
- Every post maps to a content pillar and sub-topic.
- Every post has a clear call to action (newsletter signup, related content, demo request).
- Internal linking: minimum 3 internal links per post.
- Every post is structured for scanning: subheadings every 200-300 words, bullet points where appropriate, bold for key terms.

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target (Month 1-3) | Target (Month 4-6) | Target (Month 7-12) |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Monthly organic sessions | 200-500 | 500-1,500 | 1,500-5,000 |
| Average time on page | 3-5 minutes | 3-5 minutes | 4-6 minutes |
| Blog-to-lead conversion | 1-2% | 2-3% | 3-5% |
| Backlinks earned | 2-5/month | 5-10/month | 10-20/month |
| Posts ranking page 1 | 0-2 | 3-8 | 10-20 |

**Resource allocation:** 20% of total content effort.

## Secondary Channels (Build Over Time)

These channels receive dedicated but limited resources. They amplify primary channel efforts and provide access to specific audiences that primary channels do not fully reach.

### Industry Events and Conferences

**Role in strategy:** In-person credibility building, enterprise prospect access, agency networking, and brand visibility in the baby product and marketing categories.

**Why it matters:** Enterprise Emma and Agency Alex attend industry events. A conference presence signals legitimacy. A speaking slot signals authority. These events compress months of relationship-building into days.

**Target events:**

| Event | Audience | Priority | Timing |
|-------|----------|----------|--------|
| ABC Kids Expo | Baby product brands, retailers | High | September/October |
| JPMA Baby Show | Baby product manufacturers, brands | High | Spring |
| CAGNY (Consumer Analyst Group of NY) | CPG executives, investors | Medium | February |
| Shoptalk | E-commerce, retail leaders | Medium | March |
| ANA Masters of Marketing | Senior marketers, CMOs | Medium | October |
| Advertising Week | Agencies, marketers | Medium | October |
| DTC-focused events (eTail, CommerceNext) | DTC founders, e-commerce | Medium | Varies |

**Engagement model:**
- **Year 1:** Attend 2-3 events. Focus on networking, not speaking. Learn the landscape. Identify speaking opportunities for Year 2.

- **Ongoing:** Host side events or intimate dinners at major conferences. 10-15 senior prospects in a private setting is more valuable than a booth on the expo floor.

**Cadence:** 2-3 events per quarter (attendance); 1-2 speaking engagements per quarter (when established).

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Conversations per event | 15-25 meaningful contacts |
| Meetings booked per event | 3-5 follow-up meetings |
| Pipeline influenced | $50K-$200K per event |

## Future Channels (Activate When Ready)

These channels are not active now. Each has prerequisites that must be met before activation. Do not start them prematurely -- spreading too thin too early kills momentum on primary channels.

### LinkedIn Ads (Paid Social)

**Prerequisites for activation:**
- Organic LinkedIn presence established (1,000+ followers, consistent 3%+ engagement rate)
- At least 3 high-performing organic posts to use as creative starting points
- Lead magnet or conversion asset ready (ROI calculator, compliance guide, sample imagery offer)
- Monthly budget of $3,000-$5,000 available for testing

**When to activate:** Month 4-6, once organic LinkedIn has established baseline performance and we have conversion assets.

**Expected approach:**
- Start with retargeting: serve ads to people who visited the babystudio website or engaged with LinkedIn content
- Expand to lookalike audiences based on email list and website visitors
- Content promotion: amplify top-performing organic posts to reach new audiences
- Lead generation: drive to lead magnet downloads or demo requests
- ABM (Account-Based Marketing): target specific enterprise accounts with tailored messaging

**Budget allocation:** Start at $3,000/month. Scale to $5,000-$10,000/month based on CAC and pipeline contribution.

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Cost per click | $5-$15 |
| Cost per lead | $50-$150 |
| Lead-to-meeting conversion | 10-20% |
| Pipeline per $1,000 spent | $5,000-$15,000 |

## Content Distribution Workflow

Every piece of content follows this lifecycle from creation to measurement. The workflow ensures maximum reach and impact from every investment in content production.

### Step 1: Content Creation
- Content Strategist assigns topic based on editorial calendar, keyword strategy, and pillar allocation
- Content Writer drafts the piece following brand guidelines and tone of voice
- Review and approval (CMO or Content Strategist)

### Step 2: Primary Publication
- Publish on the primary channel for that content type (blog for long-form, LinkedIn for thought leadership posts, email for outreach and nurture)
- Optimize for the primary channel (SEO for blog, character count for LinkedIn, subject line for email)

### Step 3: Cross-Channel Distribution
- Blog post published --> create 3-4 LinkedIn posts from the key insights (publish over 2 weeks)
- Blog post published --> include in next newsletter edition
- Blog post published --> add to relevant email nurture sequence
- LinkedIn post performs well --> expand into a blog post if the topic has search volume
- Email content resonates (high reply rate) --> adapt into a LinkedIn post

### Step 4: Repurposing
- Every piece of pillar content (blog posts 2,000+ words) should generate:
  - 3-5 LinkedIn posts (different angles from the same piece)
  - 1 email newsletter feature
  - 1 email nurture sequence addition
  - Pull quotes for social graphics
  - Key data points for sales materials
  - Summary points for conference talking points

### Step 5: Measurement
- Track primary metrics for each channel (see channel-specific metrics above)
- Weekly: review engagement metrics, identify top-performing content
- Monthly: review channel performance against targets, identify patterns
- Quarterly: strategic review, adjust channel allocation based on performance data

### Step 6: Optimization
- Double down on content topics and formats that outperform
- Retire or revise approaches that underperform after 60 days
- Update existing content when new data or proof points become available
- Promote evergreen content periodically -- content that performed well 3 months ago is still valuable

## Cross-Channel Integration

Channels should reinforce each other. No channel operates in isolation. The goal is that a prospect encounters babystudio's message multiple times, in multiple contexts, with increasing depth.

### Integration Patterns

**Blog + LinkedIn + Email (The Content Flywheel):**
1. Blog post published on a Pillar 1 topic (e.g., "The Compliance Tax")
2. LinkedIn post #1 (Day 0): Core insight from the blog post, standalone value
3. LinkedIn post #2 (Day 3): Different angle from the same topic
4. Newsletter (next edition): Featured insight linking to the blog post
5. LinkedIn post #3 (Day 7): Follow-up data point or reader response
6. Email nurture: Blog post added to the appropriate nurture sequence position
7. Sales: Blog post referenced in relevant sales conversations

**Events + LinkedIn + Email (The Relationship Bridge):**
1. Pre-event: LinkedIn posts about the upcoming event, themes, what we are thinking about
2. At event: Meet prospects, exchange contact information
3. Post-event (Day 1): Personalized email referencing the conversation
4. Post-event (Day 3): LinkedIn connection request with personal note
5. Post-event (Week 2): Add to nurture sequence if not yet ready for sales conversation
6. Post-event (ongoing): Engage with their LinkedIn content regularly

**Podcast + LinkedIn + Blog (The Authority Builder):**
1. Podcast appearance recorded
2. Blog recap post published (key takeaways, expanded points)
3. LinkedIn post #1: Best quote or insight from the episode
4. LinkedIn post #2: Behind-the-scenes or preparation insight
5. LinkedIn post #3: Audience question addressed (from comments)
6. Email newsletter: Feature the podcast appearance with a link
7. Pull audio clips for future social content

## Channel Performance Review Cadence

### Weekly Review
- LinkedIn: Review post performance (impressions, engagement, comments). Identify top performer. Note topics and formats that resonated.
- Email: Review open rates, reply rates, meetings booked. Identify subject lines and content that performed.
- Blog: Check traffic, time on page, conversions. Note any posts gaining organic traction.

### Monthly Review
